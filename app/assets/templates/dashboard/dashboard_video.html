Integrating Sustainability into Business Functions

Increasingly companies with experience in corporate sustainability conclude that deeper integration into their business functions and management processes is necessary to achieve greater levels of value creation, especially when shifting the focus of sustainability beyond risk management and productivity to innovating for growth. Even the best sustainability teams can only take their companies so far by themselves.


In traditional business terms, organizational integration applies to efforts that seek to continuously align the strategies, practices and policies of autonomous and independent entities to facilitate coordination, collaboration, synergies and greater innovation.


The Roadmap for Integrated Sustainability project seeks to help sustainability teams, or executive leaders responsible for sustainability goals, engage and integrate sustainability with the company’s priority business functions. The choice of priority functions and the engagement strategies differ depending on the developmental stage of sustainability and its integration in overall business strategy.


For more information on the case for functional sustainability integration see UN Global Compact Briefing Document, Introduction to Functional Integration.


Structure

The Self-Assessment tool is organized in four sections. The Functional Assessment section is designed to be completed in conversation with peers in the target function. The introductory page for each section will include further relevant instructions, brief background information, and links to more resources on that topic.


  1. Readiness Assessment - 5 questions related to the relative development and overall integration of sustainability at the corporate level.

  2. Priority Function Identification - A quick, subjective survey to help identify priority functions for deeper analysis in the last, Functional Assessment section. Sections 1 and 2 should take no more that 30 minutes to complete. Upon completion, a graphical representation of your answers will be delivered to guide selection of functions in Section 3. Section 2 must be completed before Section 3 becomes available.

  3. Functional Assessment - These questions are designed to be answered through direct discussions between sustainability leaders and peers in functions chosen with the help of the previous section. To adequately discuss the multiple choice and reflection questions, please allow at least 45 minutes for each meeting with functional peers. Please refer to the Function Briefing documents linked for each function. You may consider forwarding the relevant document as background for your functional peer discussions.

  4. Analysis and Report - Upon completion of the Self-Assessment, including each of the individual functions of your choosing, click on “CONCLUDE” in the upper right. This will “lock in” your answers. You will not be able to conclude the assessment until you have completed the Readiness and Priority Functions Assessment sections and at least three Functional Assessments. At that time, your results will be automatically generated. At any time in the process, we would welcome the opportunity to schedule a call to discuss your questions, results and feedback.
